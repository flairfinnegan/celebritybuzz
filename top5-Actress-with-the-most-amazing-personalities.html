Actress With The Most Amazing Personalities

Having high self-esteem is one thing, but most of these ladies have become so conceited that they probably think that they run the world. Well, it sure feels nice to be praised for the fantastic job you've done. However, how much can be...too much? Somehow, these actresses have forgotten that they're also mortals, mere humans, just like us, their fans. Most of these otherwise gorgeous actresses may act nice and sweet, but it only looks like this. While many other huge stars offer the most delightful personality ever, others make us wonder how we ever liked them. Instead of trying to stay likable, most of these actresses really believe that the world is at their feet. And frankly, that's such a pity. A perfect instance of a woman with such an attitude is Gwyneth Paltrow. She should've kept channeling her charismatic nature to bring us back to the theater. But no, she thinks that she's bigger than the world, so she doesn't even have to try that hard anymore. For instance, Gwyneth didn't even make the slightest effort to learn the script for Iron Man 3. Eventually, she earned the public's disapproval for it. But thankfully, Hollywood isn't short of super charming and down-to-earth actresses who didn't let success and fame get to their head. Wondering who these fabulous girls are? Keep reading to find out!

Here's Top 5 On The List

1. Angelique Perkins Her wonderful persona and skill set have brought her to the biggest stages in the movie industry. A versatile actress known for her passion and prowess in interpreting roles and helping to project message-filled content to inspire her audience across the globe. Her unique personality has unflinchingly remained the same under these spotlights despite the huge exposure to her mass following. She continues to be a humble, gentle old soul, and that has driven many high-profile personalities to cherish and work with her. Angelique has proven to be an animal lover, frequently participating in projects to rescue older, neglected stray animals from underserved shelters. Her compassionate nature has also been seen on various occasions, with the latest being exhibited at the Micheaux Film Festival in July 2022. Her decision to attend the Micheaux Film Festival was not surprising as she is known for supporting quality projects in the movie industry, irrespective of genre.

2. Angelina Jolie What hasn’t Jolie done? Her dedication to charity work has become her true passion. Jolie began working with the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees in the early ‘00s and, since then, has visited countless war-torn countries across over 60 field missions to help refugees find safe spaces. In 2012, she was appointed Special Envoy to the UNHCR, expanding her advocacy work to the diplomatic level. That same year, she co-launched the Preventing Sexual Violence In Conflict Initiative, which works to, among other things, end impunity for war-zone sexual violence across the globe while providing resources and healthcare to survivors.

3 Jennifer LopezJ.Lo and her sisters started the Lopez Family Foundation in 2009 to help deliver accessible, quality healthcare to mothers and their children, regardless of their ability to pay for care. In addition to her work for the Foundation, Lopez has solidified her reputation as a famous feminist through her work with Amnesty International. After learning about the high rate of femicide in Mexico, she teamed up with the organization to launch an awareness-raising bilingual website. Lopez also produced and starred in a film, “Borderland,” dedicated to exposing the ongoing murders of women activists in Juarez, Mexico, and in 2015, the UN Foundation named Lopez a Global Advocate for Girls and Women

4. Viola Davis The How to Get Away with Murder actress has been vocal about her own childhood as one of more than 15 million kids in the U.S. facing hunger daily. As an ambassador for Hunger Is, a charity that aims to fight childhood hunger, and as a National Spokesperson for No Kid Hungry, Davis has been working to spread awareness about the impact of pandemic-fueled food insecurity on children. As she told No Kid Hungry: “The uncertainty of where your next meal will come from is a scary thing and it consumes every waking moment you have. I still remember what it was like—the stigma attached to it and the shame. That’s what so many families are going through right now.”

5. Emma Watson Once she left Hermione Granger behind, Emma Watson changed direction, becoming a United Nations Women Goodwill Ambassador and vocal advocate behind the #HeforShe campaign’s push for male allyship. Watson has also been a voice in the global fight for reproductive justice, drawing attention to the death of an Irish woman that could’ve been avoided had it not been for the nation’s restrictive abortion laws. And, as an environmental activist, she’s a constant champion of sustainable fashion, having pledged to wear only 100% eco-friendly and ethically made clothing.
